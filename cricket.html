CRICKET

THE GENTLEMAN'S GAME

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players on a field at the centre of which is a 22-yard pitch with a wicket at each end.

BASIC INFORMATION

Number of Players

11 players per team on the field. One team bats while the other bowls and fields.

Match Duration

Test: 5 days | ODI: 50 overs (8 hours) | T20: 20 overs (3 hours)

Main Strategy

Batting partnerships, bowling variations, field placements, and run rate management.

Field Dimensions

Pitch: 22 yards (20.12m) | Boundary: 55-70m radius | Wickets: 28 inches high

CRICKET FORMATS

Test Cricket

Duration: 5 days

Overs: Unlimited

Innings: 2 per team

Highest Score: 952/6d by Sri Lanka

ODI Cricket

Duration: 1 day

Overs: 50 per side

Innings: 1 per team

Highest Score: 498/4 by England

T20 Cricket

Duration: 3 hours

Overs: 20 per side

Innings: 1 per team

Highest Score: 278/3 by Afghanistan

GAME STRATEGY

Batting Strategy

  • Powerplay: Aggressive batting in first 6 overs (T20/ODI)
  • Building Innings: Patience and strike rotation in Tests
  • Death Overs: Maximum scoring in last 4 overs
  • Partnerships: Building long batting partnerships

Bowling Strategy

  • Line & Length: Consistent bowling in good areas
  • Variations: Yorkers, slower balls, bouncers
  • Spin vs Pace: Choosing right bowlers for conditions
  • Field Placement: Setting fields for each bowler

RULES OF THE GAME

Basic Rules

  • 6 balls per over, extra ball for no-ball/wide
  • 4 runs for ball reaching boundary
  • 6 runs for ball clearing boundary on full
  • 10 ways to get out (bowled, caught, LBW, etc.)
  • Powerplay restrictions in limited overs cricket

Umpire Signals

  • Out: Raised index finger
  • Four: Arm waved side to side
  • Six: Both arms raised
  • Wide: Both arms outstretched
  • No Ball: One arm outstretched

IMPORTANT FACTS

History & Origin

  • Origin: 16th century England
  • First Test Match: 1877 (Australia vs England)
  • First World Cup: 1975 in England
  • ICC Founded: 1909 as Imperial Cricket Conference
  • First T20 World Cup: 2007 in South Africa

Major Tournaments

  • ICC Cricket World Cup: Every 4 years (ODI)
  • ICC T20 World Cup: Every 2 years
  • ICC World Test Championship: 2-year cycle
  • ICC Champions Trophy: ODI tournament
  • Indian Premier League (IPL): Annual T20 league

The Little Master

Nationality: Indian

Career Records

SWIMMING

THE ART OF AQUATIC SPEED

Swimming is an individual or team racing sport that requires the use of one's entire body to move through water. It's one of the most popular Olympic sports.

SWIMMING STROKES

Freestyle (Front Crawl)

  • Fastest swimming stroke
  • Alternating arm movements
  • Flutter kick
  • Side breathing
  • Used in freestyle events

Backstroke

  • Swum on the back
  • Alternating arm movements
  • Flutter kick
  • No breathing restrictions
  • Flags for flip turn timing

Breaststroke

  • Oldest swimming stroke
  • Synchronous arm movements
  • Whip kick (frog kick)
  • Head must break surface each stroke
  • Timing is crucial

Butterfly

  • Most physically demanding
  • Synchronous arm recovery
  • Dolphin kick
  • Both arms must clear water together
  • Requires excellent timing

RULES & TECHNIQUES

Competition Rules

  • False start results in disqualification
  • Must touch wall at each turn and finish
  • Underwater dolphin kick limits (15m)
  • Stroke technique must comply with rules
  • Lane assignments by seed times

Starts & Turns

  • Forward Start: Freestyle, breaststroke, butterfly
  • Backstroke Start: In water with feet on wall
  • Flip Turn: Freestyle and backstroke
  • Open Turn: Breaststroke and butterfly
  • Relay Exchange: Touch timing crucial

ATHLETICS

THE MOTHER OF ALL SPORTS

Athletics is one of the oldest and most fundamental sports that challenges human physical capabilities through running, jumping, and throwing events.

BASIC INFORMATION

Number of Players

Athletics is primarily an individual sport. Each event features one athlete competing at a time, but major tournaments involve hundreds of athletes.

Team Game?

Primarily individual, but some events like relay races involve teamwork. International competitions also feature team scoring systems.

Main Strategy

Pacing, energy conservation, technique optimization, and mental focus. Each event requires specific strategic approaches.

Competition Format

Heats, semifinals, and finals system. Field events have qualifying rounds followed by finals with multiple attempts.

GAME STRATEGY

Running Events

  • Pacing: Even distribution of speed versus negative/positive splits
  • Lane Selection: Inner lanes for shorter races, outer for longer
  • Final Kick: Timing the acceleration at the finish
  • Overtaking: Strategic positioning during races

Jumping Events

  • Approach Run: Building optimal speed for takeoff
  • Takeoff Angle: 20-25° for long jump, 45° for high jump
  • In-Air Technique: Fosbury flop, hitch-kick, etc.
  • Landing: Maximizing distance without fault

Throwing Events

  • Momentum Building: Glide, spin, or rotational techniques
  • Release Angle: 34-45° depending on implement
  • Follow-through: Maintaining balance after release
  • Grip: Proper hold for maximum control

RULES OF THE GAME

General Rules

  • False starts result in disqualification (1 false start rule)
  • Lane discipline must be maintained in track events
  • Implements must land within sector lines in throwing events
  • Jumpers must take off from behind the board/foul line
  • Relay baton must be passed within the exchange zone

Measurement Rules

  • All measurements taken to nearest centimeter below
  • Wind assistance limit: +2.0 m/s for records
  • Three attempts in field events, top 8 get 3 more
  • Starting blocks mandatory for races up to 400m

Competition Rules

  • Heats and finals system for track events
  • Qualifying standards for major championships
  • Anti-doping regulations strictly enforced
  • Uniform rules: National/team colors, bib numbers

IMPORTANT FACTS

History & Origin

  • First Recorded: Ancient Olympic Games, 776 BC
  • Modern Athletics: Started in 19th century England
  • First Modern Olympics: Athens 1896
  • IAAF Founded: 1912 (now World Athletics)

Major Tournaments

  • Olympic Games: Every 4 years
  • World Athletics Championships: Biennial
  • World Indoor Championships: Every 2 years
  • Diamond League: Annual series of 14 meets
  • World Cross Country Championships: Annual

USAIN BOLT

The Fastest Man Alive

Nationality: Jamaican

Born: August 21, 1986

Height: 1.95m (6'5")

Weight: 94kg (207 lbs)

Events: 100m, 200m, 4×100m relay

World Records: 100m (9.58s), 200m (19.19s)

Career Highlights

  • Only sprinter to win Olympic 100m & 200m titles at three consecutive Olympics
  • First man to win six Olympic gold medals in sprinting
  • Holds the world record in 100m, 200m and 4×100m relay
  • Named World Athlete of the Year 6 times
  • Considered the greatest sprinter of all time

SHELLY-ANN FRASER-PRYCE

Pocket Rocket

Nationality: Jamaican

Born: December 27, 1986

Height: 1.52m (5'0")

Weight: 52kg (115 lbs)

Events: 100m, 200m

Personal Best: 100m (10.60s), 200m (21.79s)

Career Highlights

  • First female sprinter to win five World Championships 100m titles
  • Oldest woman to win World Championships 100m title (2022 at age 35)
  • Only female sprinter to win 100m gold at World Championships after having a child
  • Third fastest woman in history over 100m
  • Named World Female Athlete of the Year 3 times

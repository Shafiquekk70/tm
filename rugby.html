RUGBY

THE GENTLEMAN'S GAME PLAYED BY BARBARIANS

Rugby is a contact team sport that originated in England in the first half of the 19th century. It is based on running with the ball in hand.

RUGBY VARIATIONS

Rugby Union

  • 15 players per team
  • 80 minutes (2×40 min halves)
  • Points: Try (5), Conversion (2), Penalty (3), Drop Goal (3)
  • Rucks, mauls, lineouts, scrums
  • Professional and amateur levels

Rugby League

  • 13 players per team
  • 80 minutes (2×40 min halves)
  • Points: Try (4), Conversion (2), Penalty (2), Drop Goal (1)
  • 6-tackle rule (play-the-ball)
  • Primarily professional

BASIC INFORMATION

Players

Forwards (1-8): Props, Hooker, Locks, Flankers, No. 8 | Backs (9-15): Scrum-half, Fly-half, Centers, Wings, Fullback

Field Dimensions

Field: 100m long × 70m wide | In-goal area: 10-22m deep | Goalposts: H-shaped, 5.6m wide

Strategy

Set pieces, phase play, kicking tactics, defensive patterns, ball retention, territorial gain.

Basic Rules

Forward pass not allowed | Must release ball when tackled | Offside line at rucks/mauls | Knock-on results in scrum

SCORING METHODS

Rugby Union Scoring

  • Try: 5 points (touching ball down in opponent's in-goal)
  • Conversion: 2 points (kick after try)
  • Penalty Kick: 3 points (for infringements)
  • Drop Goal: 3 points (drop kick during play)
  • Penalty Try: 7 points (automatic try + conversion)

Set Pieces

  • Scrum: Restart after minor infringement
  • Lineout: Restart after ball goes out
  • Ruck: Contest for ball on ground
  • Maul: Contest for ball with players standing

PLAYER POSITIONS

Forwards (1-8)

  • Props (1,3): Scrum power, lifting in lineouts
  • Hooker (2): Throws in lineouts, hooks ball in scrum
  • Locks (4,5): Jumpers in lineouts, scrum power
  • Flankers (6,7): Ball winners, tacklers
  • No. 8 (8): Controls ball at scrum base, ball carrier

Backs (9-15)

  • Scrum-half (9): Link between forwards and backs
  • Fly-half (10): Playmaker, decision maker
  • Centers (12,13): Attack and defense
  • Wings (11,14): Speed, finishing tries
  • Fullback (15): Last line of defense

MAJOR TOURNAMENTS

Rugby World Cup

Frequency: Every 4 years

First: 1987 (New Zealand & Australia)

Most Wins: New Zealand (3), South Africa (4)

Format: 20 teams, pool stage then knockout

Six Nations

Teams: England, Ireland, Scotland, Wales, France, Italy

Format: Annual tournament

Trophy: Triple Crown, Grand Slam, Calcutta Cup

Rugby Championship

Teams: New Zealand, Australia, South Africa, Argentina

Formerly: Tri Nations (1996-2011)

Format: Home and away matches

British & Irish Lions

Teams: Combined team from UK & Ireland

Tours: Every 4 years to Southern Hemisphere

Opponents: New Zealand, South Africa, Australia

The Greatest Fly-Half

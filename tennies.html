SCORING SYSTEM

Point System

  • Love: 0 points
  • 15: First point
  • 30: Second point
  • 40: Third point
  • Game: Win 4 points with 2-point lead
  • Deuce: 40-40, need 2 consecutive points to win
  • Advantage: Point after deuce

Match Structure

  • Set: First to 6 games with 2-game lead
  • Tiebreak: At 6-6, first to 7 points with 2-point lead
  • Match: Best of 3 or 5 sets
  • Grand Slam: Men play best of 5 sets

TENNIS SHOTS

Basic Shots

  • Serve: Starts the point
  • Forehand: Dominant hand swing
  • Backhand: One or two-handed
  • Volley: Hit before ball bounces
  • Smash: Overhead attacking shot
  • Drop Shot: Soft shot just over net
  • Lob: High defensive shot

Spin Types

  • Topspin: Ball dips quickly
  • Slice: Backspin, stays low
  • Flat: No spin, fast pace
  • Kick Serve: High bouncing serve

TENNIS COURT SURFACES

Hard Court

  • Speed: Medium to fast
  • Bounce: True and consistent
  • Tournaments: Australian Open, US Open
  • Advantage: All-round players
  • Disadvantage: Hard on joints

Clay Court

  • Speed: Slow
  • Bounce: High and slow
  • Tournaments: French Open
  • Advantage: Baseline players
  • Disadvantage: Bad bounce in wet conditions

Grass Court

  • Speed: Very fast
  • Bounce: Low and unpredictable
  • Tournaments: Wimbledon
  • Advantage: Serve & volley players
  • Disadvantage: Unpredictable bounces

Carpet Court

  • Speed: Very fast
  • Bounce: Low and even
  • Tournaments: Indoor tournaments
  • Advantage: Aggressive players
  • Disadvantage: Less common now
